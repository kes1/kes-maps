Orrin
River Orrin
 

I have included two routes from the upper and lower sections of this river :

Route 1 :

This is more a proposed ride through the clash woods ( FC owned ). It will last about 20+ min.depending upon how adventurous you are. The main track is of a high quality, although the North down from the single track ( Aultgowrie ) road is rather steep and slippy at times, watch out for the dip at the bottom. You can go right down to the river, although you do have to cross the shallow stream, where the track from the road meets and then walk along underneath the electricity pylons. You can, instead of going on the small road joining the the main tracks, take any of the numerous paths up the hill on the South and then follow these - there is a pretty high chance youll get where youre heading. This is a nice we jaunt - Sunday afternoon sort, but watch out for folk walking their dogs as these woods are a popular getaway. Park ref. NH513516

Route 2 :

Another nice ride which goes through the grounds of a working estate. Park at the blue blob and put some money in the donation box provided (to a local charity) should you wish. Follow the tarmac road up and cross the bridge (with a wooden walkway) over the river, and turning right to head back downstream. You will pass the Orrin falls, although to view them best you need to get off your bike and wander down to them, youll be able to hear where they are ! In season you can see fish leaping the falls. Once you reach the gatehouse turn right onto the road and you are back at the start. For the more adventurous the extended section is a tarmac road which goes all the way up to the reservoir, a short ride for the views, depending how fit you are as there is a lot of climbing involved. The first time I went up to the reservoir it took me a good hour, although I can now do it in 20min. If you seek an even longer cycle ( a whole day) you could follow this track, which deteriorates to gravel although is still a good stable surface, all the way to Erchless castle [NH4141]. From here you could then cycle back down the glen to Beauly. I would recommend if you were to follow this route that you plan and choose your day carefully, especially to avoid winds which can be much stronger on high ground. Park ref. NH476516
