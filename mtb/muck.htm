Muckernish woods
This is a short (4-5 miles) easy route, on a relatively stable surface, although parts can get rather muddy. You can do a round trip two ways, with one being half on road.

There is space to park a couple of cars at the start point, although please do not block the gate. To reach it follow the A835 from either Tore or Maryburgh, approx 2 and 5 miles respectively. Follow the Red packed gravel path as shown on the map for about 3/4 mile, after which you will reach a fork. You can choose either way, I will give directions for straight on. After another mile you will come to a clearing where a couple of overgrown tracks break off - it is not that obvious which is which but you want to follow the hairpin right round and stay on the main track. Follow the track until just after the first large puddle, where you want to take the turning on the left - just before the small hill. You then follow this track back round to the fork - it is pretty straightforward. The second part can get very muddy so you might prefer to continue on the main track - after which you will come to a T-junction with a farm road (just after passing through another gate) turn left here and you will come to the single track road round the corner. Follow the tarred road down to the B9169, where again you want to turn left towards Easter Kinkell. After about 10 min. steady cycling you will come to a junction with the A835, if you want to return to the starting point turn left. Otherwise right takes you to Dingwall.

 

NEWS During summer '99 these woods have been thinned, and as such access was restricted during the summer months, with some trails closed. I have been unable to visit thewoods recently so cannot tell whether they are now open again. E-mail me from the comments page if you know otherwise.

The start point is NH578525 on the OS Landranger sheet 26.
